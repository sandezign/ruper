# Mini Check Sample Report Design

**Status:** Approved for specification  
**Date:** 2026-06-07  
**Primary language:** Bahasa Indonesia  
**Applies to:** Customer-facing Mini Check sample report

## 1. Objective

Create a complete, customer-facing Mini Check sample that demonstrates how
Rumper communicates an initial location assessment using only Mini Check
priority parameters.

The sample must be concise enough to read through WhatsApp, while still making
the score basis, evidence limitations, confidence, and required field
validation clear.

## 2. Approved Example Scenario

The report uses the fictional location:

`Perumahan Taman Arunika, Bekasi`

The location and all findings are fictional. The report must display this
clearly so the sample cannot be interpreted as a real assessment.

The example has the following analytical profile:

- overall Mini Check score of approximately `68/100`;
- outcome label `Layak dengan catatan`;
- commute and essential amenities are generally supporting;
- an indication of recurring access-road ponding requires validation;
- the primary access route has a single-access bottleneck;
- no confirmed critical red flag;
- primary recommendation: proceed with caution after flood and access
  validation.

## 3. Customer-Facing Positioning

The report is an initial screening, not a Full Location Risk Report.

It must state:

- the score uses only Mini Check priority parameters;
- the result is not equivalent to complete location due diligence;
- missing information is not treated as a neutral or good result;
- the customer must validate material findings before paying a booking fee or
  down payment.

## 4. Score Presentation

The sample displays:

- a temporary overall Mini Check score;
- five temporary category scores;
- category coverage;
- category confidence;
- visible limitations where evidence remains incomplete.

The report must not imply that the temporary overall score is interchangeable
with the complete RLS produced by a Full Report.

## 5. Report Structure

The sample is organized as a mobile-readable report of approximately 4-5
pages:

1. **Cover and Mini Check limitations**
   - fictional sample label;
   - property reference;
   - report ID, version, and date;
   - purpose and limitations.
2. **Decision summary**
   - temporary overall score;
   - outcome label;
   - primary recommendation;
   - top supporting factors;
   - top concerns requiring validation.
3. **Five-category score summary**
   - temporary score;
   - coverage;
   - confidence;
   - short rationale for every category.
4. **Material findings**
   - observation;
   - evidence summary;
   - confidence;
   - evidence limitations;
   - implication for the buyer.
5. **Field-validation checklist and conclusion**
   - specific questions and observations;
   - decision gate before booking fee or DP;
   - recommended next steps;
   - disclaimer.

## 6. Planned Category Profile

The complete sample will use category values that produce an overall score of
approximately `68/100` under the existing category weights.

| Category | Intended profile |
|---|---|
| Flood resilience | Needs attention due to indicated access-road ponding |
| Commute efficiency | Generally supporting with a recurring peak-hour bottleneck |
| Physical access and roads | Needs attention due to a single primary access route |
| Essential amenities | Generally supporting for common daily needs |
| Environmental quality and red flags | No confirmed critical issue, but selected conditions still require field validation |

Exact category values, coverage, and confidence labels will be selected during
sample-report implementation and must remain internally consistent with the
written findings.

## 7. Evidence Presentation

Because the case is fictional, evidence references must use clearly fictional
source labels rather than real URLs or claims.

Each material finding must include:

- observation or claim;
- fictional evidence summary;
- evidence date where relevant;
- relevance to the candidate point;
- confidence label;
- limitation;
- validation action.

No fictional source may be presented as a real authority, publication, or
verified dataset.

## 8. Recommendation Logic

The report recommendation is:

`Lanjut dengan hati-hati setelah validasi banjir dan akses`

The recommendation follows from:

- a temporary overall score in the `Layak dengan catatan` range;
- supporting commute and amenity findings;
- material uncertainty around access-road ponding;
- a single-access bottleneck;
- no confirmed critical override.

The objective score and the recommendation must remain visually and
conceptually separate.

## 9. Validation Checklist Requirements

The sample checklist must direct the customer to:

- visit during or shortly after heavy rain;
- inspect the indicated access-road ponding point;
- ask multiple independent residents about frequency, depth, and duration;
- test entry and exit during peak hours;
- confirm whether an alternative emergency route exists;
- verify travel time to the primary activity destination;
- inspect noise, odor, road safety, and environmental conditions;
- record photos, timestamps, and answers for later review.

## 10. Acceptance Criteria

The sample is complete when:

1. It is clearly labeled as fictional.
2. It uses only Mini Check priority parameters.
3. It displays temporary category scores, coverage, confidence, and a
   temporary overall score.
4. The score calculation matches the existing RLS category weights.
5. Findings, confidence, limitations, and validation actions are consistent.
6. The recommendation follows from the score and material findings.
7. No critical red flag is stated as confirmed.
8. The report can be understood without analyst explanation.
9. The report is concise and mobile-readable.
10. The report includes a clear disclaimer and decision gate before payment.
