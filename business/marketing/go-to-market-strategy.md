# Rumper Go-to-Market Strategy

## Document Purpose

This document defines Rumper's validation-first go-to-market strategy for the first 90 days. It is the master marketing strategy for acquiring early customers, validating willingness to pay, and proving a repeatable path to paid location-risk audits.

Rumper should optimize for learning and paid evidence before broad awareness or scale.

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Product Overview

Rumper is an independent buyer-side service that helps first-time homebuyers check location risks before paying a booking fee or down payment.

The concierge MVP converts fragmented location information into a clear report covering:

- Flood risk
- Real commute
- Road access
- Facilities and surrounding environmental red flags
- Evidence and confidence labels
- A proceed, caution, verify further, or avoid recommendation
- A tailored physical-survey checklist

### 1.2 GTM Objective

The first 90 days should prove that Rumper can repeatedly:

1. Reach first-time homebuyers who have a real shortlisted property.
2. Create enough trust for them to submit its location.
3. Convert qualified leads into paid location-risk audits.
4. Deliver useful reports within viable time and cost limits.
5. Identify the strongest segment, message, offer, and acquisition channel.

Broad awareness, large-scale paid acquisition, and full monetization are not initial priorities.

### 1.3 Strategic Thesis

Rumper can win early customers by owning the decision moment immediately before booking fee or DP.

Property portals help buyers discover homes. Agents and developers help close transactions. Rumper protects the buyer's decision by independently checking location risks before commitment.

The core GTM hypothesis is:

> First-time homebuyers with a real shortlisted property will submit its location and pay for an independent audit when Rumper connects location risk to their immediate fear of losing money, suffering a difficult daily commute, or regretting a rushed decision.

Trust is the critical constraint. Rumper must demonstrate independence, useful evidence, transparent limitations, and decision clarity.

## 2. Market & Customer Context

### 2.1 Market Problem

- Property information is usually designed to support a sale.
- Important location risks are difficult for inexperienced buyers to verify.
- Relevant data is fragmented across maps, government sources, news, social media, community discussions, and field observations.
- Marketing claims such as `bebas banjir`, `strategis`, and `dekat stasiun` often lack clear evidence.
- A wrong location decision can create years of financial cost, commuting stress, and regret.

### 2.2 Target Audience

The initial audience is first-time homebuyers who:

- Are approximately 25–40 years old
- Live or work in Jabodetabek
- Are buying a home to occupy
- Use KPR or long-term savings
- Have limited experience evaluating property locations
- Need evidence to support a personal or family decision

### 2.3 Primary Use Case

The primary user has shortlisted one to three homes and expects to pay a booking fee or DP within one to three months. They want to validate location risks before making a difficult-to-reverse financial commitment.

## 3. Positioning Strategy

### 3.1 Category Positioning

Rumper is **buyer-side location-risk intelligence for residential property decisions**.

In consumer communication, this category should be simplified to **cek risiko lokasi rumah**.

Rumper is not a property portal, sales agent, building inspector, or technical GIS platform.

### 3.2 Positioning Statement

> For first-time homebuyers who are afraid of making the wrong decision, Rumper is an independent service that checks location risks before booking fee or down payment, so they can buy with greater confidence and less regret.

### 3.3 Core Value Proposition

**Property platforms help sell and find homes. Rumper helps buyers feel more confident choosing the right location.**

Brand promise: **Lebih yakin sebelum DP.**

### 3.4 Differentiation

| Alternative | Primary role | Rumper difference |
|---|---|---|
| Property portal | Helps users find listings | Evaluates hidden risks after a buyer has candidates |
| Property agent or developer | Helps close a transaction | Provides an independent buyer-side perspective |
| Google Maps | Shows locations, distances, and routes | Converts multiple signals into practical risk findings |
| GIS software | Enables technical spatial analysis | Produces a consumer-friendly decision recommendation |
| Home inspector | Checks building condition | Checks the surrounding location and daily-life risks |

Rumper's differentiators are independence, pre-DP timing, explainable evidence, actionable recommendations, and a report that supports family decision-making.

## 4. ICP & Segment Prioritization

### 4.1 Primary ICP

|---|---|---|---|

- Plans to decide within three months

- Has influence over the purchase decision

## 5. Messaging Strategy

### 5.1 Main Campaign Message

**Rumahnya bagus. Risikonya sudah dicek?**

Primary action message: **Sebelum DP, cek dulu risiko lokasinya.**

### 5.2 Supporting Messages

- Brosur menunjukkan kelebihan. Rumper membantu melihat risikonya.
- Rumah bisa direnovasi. Lokasi sulit diubah.
- Dekat di peta belum tentu nyaman dijalani.
- Bebas banjir menurut sales belum tentu bebas risiko.
- Keputusan besar membutuhkan bukti, bukan hanya janji.

### 5.3 Message Pillars

1. **Fear of regret:** prevent a rushed, difficult-to-reverse decision.
2. **Flood-risk verification:** test claims using available evidence.
3. **Commute reality:** expose the real cost in time, effort, and transport.
4. **Access and environmental red flags:** reveal risks missed during a short survey.
5. **Family decision support:** provide a neutral framework for discussion.

All communication should use firm preventive hooks followed by calm, objective guidance.

## 6. Offer Strategy

### 6.1 Pre-MVP Offer

**Free Mini Location Risk Check for the first 20 qualified users**

The mini-check should reveal one or two meaningful findings, show Rumper's evidence format, and recommend what the buyer should verify next. Its scope and availability must remain limited so it does not replace the paid offer.

### 6.2 MVP Offer

**Rumper Full Location Risk Report**

- Initial beta price: Rp99,000–Rp149,000
- Intended later AOV: at least Rp300,000 through improved packaging
- Delivery: concise PDF report and optional short explanation
- Core output: risk findings, evidence, confidence labels, final recommendation, and survey checklist

### 6.3 Future Offer

- Side-by-side comparison for two or three candidate homes
- Premium consultation
- Specialized commute or flood deep-dive
- Referral package for independent KPR advisors and financial planners
- Self-serve location-risk product after concierge validation gates pass

## 7. Channel Strategy

Each channel must be evaluated by qualified locations and paid customers, not reach or lead volume alone.

### 7.1 Organic Educational Content

**Role:** Build problem awareness, explain the category, and establish founder trust.

**Channels:** Instagram Reels, TikTok, carousels, LinkedIn founder posts, and anonymized case breakdowns.

**Initial test:** Publish 12–15 pieces over two weeks using pre-DP, flood, and commute angles.

### 7.2 Property Communities

**Role:** Learn buyer language, recruit interviews, and acquire high-context early users.

Use educational discussions, polls, and limited research invitations across relevant Facebook, WhatsApp, Telegram, and property communities. Avoid hard selling and respect community rules.

### 7.3 Micro-Partnerships

**Role:** Borrow trust from people already advising buyers near commitment.

Priority partners:

- Independent KPR advisors
- Family financial planners
- Buyer-friendly property educators
- First-home communities
- Relevant micro creators

Test co-branded checklists, educational sessions, sample reports, and tracked referrals.

### 7.4 Limited Paid Ads

**Role:** Compare messages and offers quickly, not scale acquisition.

- Platform: Meta Ads
- Initial budget: Rp100,000–Rp150,000 per day for seven days
- Test angles: pre-DP anxiety, flood fear, and commute pain
- Optimize for qualified real-location submissions
- Pause tests that produce cheap but unqualified leads

## 10. Landing Page Strategy

### 10.1 Landing Page Goal

Convert relevant traffic into qualified leads who submit a real property location and decision context.

### 10.2 Recommended Structure

1. Hero: `Cek risiko lokasi rumah sebelum bayar DP.`
2. Problem: explain why a good-looking home can still have a problematic location.
3. What Rumper checks: flood, commute, access, and red flags.
4. Sample report: show findings, evidence, confidence labels, and recommendation.
5. Trust: explain that Rumper is independent and buyer-side.
6. FAQ: clarify scope, limitations, process, and pricing.
7. CTA: request a location-risk check.

### 10.3 Lead Form Fields

- Name
- WhatsApp number
- Property area
- Google Maps link, listing link, or property name
- Whether the user has a specific candidate
- Expected booking-fee or DP timeline
- Primary location concern
- Whether the home is intended for occupancy

## 11. Activation & WhatsApp Flow

### 11.1 Lead Qualification

Confirm:

1. Is the property intended for occupancy?
2. Does the lead have a specific location?
3. When is the booking-fee or DP decision?
4. What risk concerns them most?
5. Who else is involved in the decision?

Prioritize leads with a real location, a decision within three months, and a specific concern.

### 11.2 Mini-Check Delivery

Deliver:

- Top one to three initial findings
- Initial risk label and confidence level
- Evidence preview
- Recommended field-verification questions
- Clear next-step recommendation

Ask which finding was most useful and what remains unclear.

### 11.3 Upsell Flow

1. Connect the mini-check finding to the buyer's decision.
2. Explain what the full report adds.
3. Show a sample report or relevant section.
4. Present beta pricing and delivery time.
5. Ask whether the buyer wants to continue.
6. Record the reason for purchase, delay, or rejection.

## 12. Experiment Plan

### 12.1 Core Experiments

| Hypothesis | Test | Success signal |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-DP anxiety produces the most qualified leads | Compare pre-DP, flood, and commute hooks | Highest qualified-location rate |
| A concrete mini-check converts better than a waitlist | A/B primary landing-page CTA | Higher qualified lead conversion |
| A sample report increases trust | Add report preview to landing page | Higher real-location submission rate |
| WhatsApp qualification improves paid conversion | Use a consistent qualification script | Reply rate above 50% and paid intent |
| Micro-partners produce high-intent leads | Test three partner activations | Qualified referrals and paid conversion |
| Buyers will pay for a full audit | Offer paid audit after mini-check | Paid purchases at target pricing |
| A comparison package raises AOV | Offer it to buyers with two candidates | Higher AOV without lower close rate |

### 12.2 Experiment Prioritization

Prioritize experiments using:

- **Impact:** potential effect on paid validation
- **Confidence:** strength of current evidence
- **Ease:** time, cost, and operational effort
- **Learning value:** ability to resolve a critical uncertainty

Every experiment must record audience, hypothesis, message, offer, spend or effort, leads, qualified leads, real locations, paid conversions, result, and decision.

## 15. Risks & Mitigation

### 15.1 Message Too Broad

**Risk:** General property messaging creates weak urgency and unqualified leads.

**Mitigation:** Focus on buyers with real candidates near DP and lead with flood, commute, access, or a specific decision risk.

### 15.2 User Thinks Rumper Is a Property Agent

**Risk:** Buyers distrust Rumper or misunderstand its role.

**Mitigation:** Consistently state that Rumper is independent, buyer-side, and has no incentive to close the property transaction.

### 15.3 Low Willingness to Pay

**Risk:** Buyers believe the service only provides information they can find for free.

**Mitigation:** Demonstrate personalized synthesis, evidence quality, time saved, hidden findings, and actionable recommendations through sample reports and mini-checks.

### 15.4 Manual Report Takes Too Long

**Risk:** The service cannot reach viable margins or delivery speed.

**Mitigation:** Limit MVP scope, standardize sources and templates, measure production time, and improve the workflow after every report.

### 15.5 Claims Sound Too Absolute

**Risk:** Overstated claims damage trust and create false expectations.

**Mitigation:** Use confidence labels, state evidence gaps, separate facts from analyst judgment, and never guarantee safety or investment performance.

## 16. Decision Gates

### 16.1 After 30 Days

Continue when there are:

- At least 20 leads
- At least 10 qualified leads
- At least 5 real locations
- Paid beta purchases or credible willingness-to-pay signals

If engagement is high but location submissions are low, improve trust and proof. If leads are unqualified, narrow targeting and messaging.

### 16.2 After 60 Days

Continue when:

- Qualified leads convert into paid beta customers
- Reports can be delivered consistently
- Customers confirm the report improves their decision
- One or more acquisition channels show promising qualified conversion

If users value reports but do not pay, revise packaging, proof, and pricing before increasing acquisition.

### 16.3 After 90 Days

Scale only when Rumper has:

- At least 10 paid reports
- A clearly validated priority segment and pain point
- At least one repeatable acquisition channel
- CAC below Rp150,000
- AOV of at least Rp300,000
- Report production time below 2.5 hours
- At least 80% of customers rating the service above 8/10

Missing the gate requires focused iteration on the weakest constraint before scaling.

## 17. Final Strategic Focus

The GTM strategy must answer one central question:

> Are first-time homebuyers sufficiently concerned about choosing the wrong location that they will submit a real property location and pay for an independent risk report before DP?

If the answer is supported by repeated paid behavior, acceptable acquisition economics, and viable report delivery, Rumper can move from a concierge MVP toward a self-serve location-risk product.
