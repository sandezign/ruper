# Rumper Go-to-Market Strategy

## Document Purpose

This document defines Rumper's practical 90-day go-to-market strategy for validating demand, acquiring early customers, and proving a repeatable path to paid location-risk audits.

The strategy is deliberately validation-first. Rumper should not optimize for broad awareness until it proves that first-time homebuyers will submit a real property location, discuss their decision context, receive a report, and pay for buyer-side location-risk advice.

## Strategic Summary

| Item | Decision |
|---|---|
| Market | First-time homebuyers in Jabodetabek |
| Beachhead segment | Buyers with one to three shortlisted homes who expect to pay a booking fee or DP within one to three months |
| Priority personas | Almost-DP Couple and Commuter First Buyer |
| Category | Buyer-side location-risk intelligence |
| Brand promise | Lebih yakin sebelum DP. |
| Core campaign | Sebelum DP, cek dulu lokasinya. |

| Primary acquisition motion | Educational content, property communities, micro-partnerships, and tightly controlled paid-message tests |
| Primary conversion event | Qualified buyer submits a real location and completes WhatsApp qualification |
| 90-day objective | Prove a repeatable segment, message, channel, offer, and delivery model that reaches the formal validation gates |

## GTM Thesis

Rumper will win early customers by owning the decision moment immediately before booking fee or down payment.

Property portals help buyers discover homes. Agents and developers help close transactions. Rumper helps buyers independently evaluate whether a shortlisted home's location is suitable before they commit.

The initial GTM hypothesis is:

> First-time homebuyers with a real shortlisted property will submit its location and pay for an independent audit when the message connects location risk to their immediate fear of losing money, suffering a difficult commute, or regretting a rushed decision.

The first 90 days must validate:

1. The audience understands Rumper within 5–10 seconds.
2. The pre-DP moment creates sufficient urgency.
3. Buyers trust Rumper enough to submit a real location.
4. The report creates value beyond information buyers believe they can find themselves.
5. Buyers will pay at a price and acquisition cost that support the business.

## Beachhead Customer

### Primary Segment

First-time homebuyers aged approximately 25–40 who live or work in Jabodetabek, are buying a home to occupy, have shortlisted one to three properties, and expect to pay a booking fee or DP within one to three months.

This segment is the strongest starting point because it combines a specific property, an urgent decision, a high perceived cost of error, and a clear reason to seek independent evidence.

### Segment Priority

| Priority | Segment | Trigger | Core pain | Message angle |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Almost-DP Couple | Sales asks for booking fee or DP | Fear of making an expensive decision and disappointing family | Sebelum bayar DP, cek apakah lokasinya benar-benar layak. |
| 2 | Commuter First Buyer | Comparing affordable homes in satellite cities | Uncertainty about daily travel time, effort, and cost | Murah di harga belum tentu murah di waktu dan energi. |
| 3 | Flood-Anxious Buyer | Considering an area with flood or inundation concerns | Distrust of `bebas banjir` claims | Klaim bebas banjir perlu bukti, bukan janji. |
| 4 | Family-Influenced Buyer | Family members disagree about a property | Lack of a neutral decision framework | Gunakan bukti yang sama untuk diskusi bersama keluarga. |
| 5 | Budget-Constrained Buyer | Finds an attractive low-priced property | Fear of hidden location costs | Rumah murah bisa menjadi mahal karena lokasi. |

### Qualification Criteria

A qualified lead should meet at least three of these criteria:

- Has a real property address, listing link, or map pin
- Plans to decide within three months
- Is buying a first home to occupy
- Can describe a specific concern such as flood, commute, access, or surrounding environment
- Is willing to continue through WhatsApp
- Has influence over the purchase decision

## Positioning and Messaging

### Positioning Statement

For first-time homebuyers who are afraid of making the wrong decision, Rumper is an independent service that checks location risks before booking fee or down payment, so they can buy with greater confidence and less regret.

### Messaging Hierarchy

| Level | Message |
|---|---|
| Brand promise | Lebih yakin sebelum DP. |
| Category | Cek risiko lokasi rumah |
| Campaign hook | Rumahnya bagus. Risikonya sudah dicek? |
| Core benefit | Membantu pembeli melihat risiko sebelum membuat komitmen besar |
| Functional proof | Cek banjir, commute, akses jalan, fasilitas, dan red flag lokasi |
| Trust proof | Independen, berpihak pada pembeli, dan berbasis bukti |
| Action | Cek dulu sebelum booking fee atau DP |

### Message Principles

- Lead with the buyer's decision and risk, not GIS or technical methodology.
- Create urgency without exaggerating danger.
- Challenge unverifiable claims, not agents or developers as a group.
- Show the report and evidence so the service does not look like generic free information.
- State uncertainty and data limitations clearly to protect trust.

### Message Tests

| Angle | Hook | Intended learning |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-DP anxiety | Sebelum bayar DP rumah, cek dulu risiko lokasinya. | Does proximity to commitment produce the most qualified leads? |
| Flood fear | Bebas banjir menurut sales belum tentu bebas risiko. | Is flood the strongest entry point? |
| Commute cost | Rumahnya dekat di peta, tapi commute-nya sanggup? | Does daily-life cost resonate with suburban buyers? |
| Buyer independence | Brosur menunjukkan kelebihan. Rumper membantu melihat risikonya. | Does independent buyer-side positioning increase trust? |
| Irreversibility | Rumah bisa direnovasi. Lokasi sulit diubah. | Does a broad risk frame improve awareness and recall? |

## Offer and Funnel

### Offer Ladder

| Stage | Offer | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Educational content and checklist | Make location due diligence feel necessary |
| Lead capture | Limited free mini location-risk check | Reduce friction and test trust |
| Core conversion | Paid concierge location-risk audit | Validate willingness to pay and report value |
| Expansion | Dual-location comparison and short consultation | Increase AOV and support harder decisions |
| Proof loop | Anonymized case study and referral request | Build trust and lower future acquisition cost |

The free mini-check must be limited and intentionally incomplete. It should reveal one or two meaningful findings, demonstrate the evidence format, and create a reason to purchase the full audit. It must not replace the paid product.

### Funnel

| Stage | Required user action | Primary metric |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Views relevant content or partner recommendation | Qualified reach |
| Interest | Visits landing page or views sample report | Click-through rate |
| Lead | Starts form or WhatsApp conversation | Lead conversion rate |
| Qualification | Submits real location and decision context | Qualified lead rate |
| Delivery | Receives mini-check or audit | Completion and production time |
| Value | Reviews findings and uses checklist or shares report | Satisfaction and report usefulness |
| Revenue | Pays for audit or comparison | Paid conversion, AOV, CAC |
| Referral | Recommends Rumper or permits anonymized case study | Referral rate |

### Landing Page Requirements

1. Hero: `Cek risiko lokasi rumah sebelum bayar DP.`
2. Problem: explain why a good-looking home can still have a problematic location.
3. Scope: flood, commute, road access, facilities, environmental red flags, and recommendation.
4. Sample report: show score, evidence, confidence labels, and survey checklist.
5. Trust: state that Rumper is independent and buyer-side.
6. CTA: request a location-risk check or view a sample report.
7. Qualification: collect location, purchase timeline, primary concern, and WhatsApp number.

## Channel Strategy

Rumper should concentrate on four channels during validation. Each channel has a distinct job and should be judged by qualified locations submitted, not vanity metrics.

### 1. Organic Educational Content

**Role:** Create problem awareness, explain the category, and build founder trust.

**Primary formats:** Instagram Reels, TikTok short videos, carousels, founder LinkedIn posts, report previews, and anonymized case breakdowns.

**Content pillars:**

- Before-DP checks
- Flood and environmental-risk myths
- Real commute and access costs
- Property-claim verification
- Family decision tools and anonymized cases

**First test:** Publish 12–15 pieces over two weeks using the pre-DP, flood, and commute angles. Route every piece to the same qualified-lead form.

**Continue when:** Content produces saves, shares, relevant questions, and real location submissions.

### 2. Property and KPR Communities

**Role:** Learn buyers' language, recruit interviews, and acquire high-context early users.

**Approach:** Begin with useful discussions, polls, and limited research requests. Avoid hard selling and respect each community's promotion rules.

**First test:** Post three discussion prompts across five relevant communities, then invite suitable respondents to a limited mini-check.

**Continue when:** Community conversations produce specific pain language, interviews, and qualified leads with real properties.

### 3. Micro-Partnerships

**Role:** Borrow trust from people already advising buyers near the purchase decision.

**Priority partners:** Independent KPR advisors, family financial planners, buyer-friendly property educators, first-home communities, and micro creators with relevant audiences.

**Partner offer:** Co-branded pre-DP checklist, educational session, sample report, and tracked referral to a limited mini-check or paid audit.

**First test:** Run one activation each with three partners and compare qualified referrals, conversion, and operational effort.

**Continue when:** A partner can repeatedly produce qualified leads at an acceptable cost or effort.

### 4. Limited Paid Message Testing

**Role:** Rapidly compare messages and landing-page offers, not scale acquisition.

**Setup:** Use a small Meta Ads test with separate pre-DP, flood, and commute creatives. Keep audience, landing page, and budget conditions comparable.

**First test budget:** Rp100,000–Rp150,000 per day for seven days, capped and reviewed daily.

**Continue when:** Paid traffic produces qualified leads and supports a plausible path to CAC below Rp150,000. Pause if it only produces cheap but unqualified form submissions.

## Experiment Backlog

| Priority | Hypothesis | Test | Success signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| High | The pre-DP message produces the most qualified leads | Compare three ad and organic hooks | Highest qualified-location rate |
| High | A concrete mini-check converts better than a waitlist | A/B the primary landing-page CTA | Higher qualified lead conversion |
| High | A sample report increases trust | Add report preview to landing page | Higher location submission rate |
| High | WhatsApp qualification improves paid conversion | Use a consistent qualification and follow-up script | Reply rate above 50% and paid intent |
| High | KPR advisors produce high-intent leads | Test three partner activations | Qualified referral and paid conversion |
| High | Buyers will pay for a full audit | Offer paid audit after mini-check | Paid purchases at target pricing |
| Medium | Flood is the best awareness entry point | Publish and advertise flood content | Strong reach-to-qualified-lead ratio |
| Medium | A dual-location package raises AOV | Offer comparison to buyers with two candidates | Higher AOV without lower close rate |
| Medium | Checklist content captures earlier buyers | Offer a pre-DP checklist | Leads later progress to real locations |

Every experiment must record its audience, message, offer, spend or effort, leads, qualified leads, real locations, paid conversions, and learning.

## 90-Day Execution Roadmap

Assumed start date: **Monday, June 8, 2026**.

### Phase 1: Problem, Message, and Trust Validation

**Period:** June 8–July 5, 2026

**Objective:** Prove that the target segment understands the offer and will submit real locations.

**Actions:**

- Finalize landing page, lead form, analytics, sample report, and WhatsApp qualification script.
- Interview at least 10 target buyers.
- Publish the first 30-day educational campaign.
- Participate in 5–10 relevant communities.
- Offer a limited free mini-check to the first 20 suitable users.
- Test pre-DP, flood, and commute messages.

**Early signal gate:**

- 100+ landing-page visits
- 20+ leads
- 10+ qualified leads
- 5+ real locations submitted
- 2–3 paid beta purchases or strong explicit willingness to pay

If engagement is high but location submissions are low, improve trust and proof. If leads are unqualified, narrow the message and form around the pre-DP moment.

### Phase 2: Paid Offer and Delivery Validation

**Period:** July 6–August 2, 2026

**Objective:** Convert early demand into a repeatable paid concierge service.

**Actions:**

- Launch the paid audit and dual-location comparison.
- Standardize report templates, evidence labels, delivery, and follow-up.
- Test three micro-partners.
- Run tightly capped paid-message tests.
- Add FAQs, anonymized examples, and trust explanations to the landing page.
- Measure production time and customer satisfaction for every report.

**Progress gate:**

- 50–100 cumulative leads
- 20–40 qualified leads
- 10–20 mini-checks or audits delivered
- 5–10 paid customers
- WhatsApp reply rate above 50%
- Clear evidence of the best-performing segment and message

### Phase 3: Repeatability and Unit Economics

**Period:** August 3–September 5, 2026

**Objective:** Find the strongest repeatable acquisition path and meet the repository's formal validation gates.

**Actions:**

- Concentrate on the best one or two acquisition channels.
- Formalize partner referral tracking and a lightweight partner kit.
- Test packaging and price to reach the target AOV.
- Reduce production time through templates and internal tools.
- Publish anonymized case studies and request referrals.
- Review the funnel and unit economics weekly.

**Formal validation gate:**

- At least 10 paid reports
- CAC below Rp150,000
- AOV of at least Rp300,000
- Report production time below 2.5 hours
- At least 80% of customers rate the service above 8/10

Passing the gate supports further product and acquisition investment. Missing it requires focused iteration on the weakest constraint before scaling.

## Measurement Framework

### North-Star Validation Metric

**Paid location-risk audits delivered to qualified first-time homebuyers.**

This metric combines real buyer intent, trust, willingness to pay, and delivery capability.

### KPI Scorecard

| Metric | 30-day target | 90-day target | Diagnostic use |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Landing-page visits | 100–300 | 1,000–2,500 | Distribution strength |
| Landing-page lead conversion | 5–15% | 8–18% | Offer and page clarity |
| Qualified lead rate | 30–50% | 40–60% | Targeting quality |
| Real locations submitted | 5–15 | 50–100 | Trust and intent |
| WhatsApp reply rate | 50–70% | 60–80% | Lead quality and follow-up |
| Full-report interest | 20–40% of qualified leads | 25–45% | Perceived value |
| Paid reports | 2–3 early signals | 10+ | Willingness to pay |
| CAC | Establish baseline | Below Rp150,000 | Acquisition viability |
| AOV | Test packages | At least Rp300,000 | Revenue viability |
| Production time | Below 2.5 hours | Below 2.5 hours, trending down | Operational viability |
| Customer satisfaction | Establish baseline | 80% above 8/10 | Delivered value |

### Weekly Review

Every week, review:

1. Which segment submitted the most qualified locations?
2. Which message generated the highest qualified-lead rate?
3. Which channel produced paid customers, not only leads?
4. Why did qualified buyers purchase or decline?
5. Which report findings created the most value?
6. What caused production delays?
7. What single experiment should be prioritized next?

## Operating Requirements

### Minimum GTM Assets

- Landing page with a clear pre-DP offer
- Qualified-lead form and WhatsApp handoff
- Sample mini-check and full audit
- Pricing and package explanation
- WhatsApp qualification, follow-up, and close scripts
- Partner one-pager and tracked referral links
- Content templates for four core pillars
- Experiment tracker and weekly scorecard

### Customer Data and Trust

- Do not publish or commit customer addresses, reports, phone numbers, or payment data.
- Obtain permission before using any anonymized case.
- Explain evidence sources, confidence, limitations, and report scope.
- Avoid implying certainty or guarantees about future flood, commute, or environmental conditions.

## Key Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Why it matters | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Rumper looks like free information | Buyers may not see a reason to pay | Demonstrate synthesis, evidence quality, recommendations, and personalized checks |
| The message is too broad | Low urgency produces unqualified leads | Focus on buyers with real candidates near DP |
| Fear-based marketing damages trust | Buyers may view Rumper as alarmist | Use calm evidence, confidence labels, and balanced recommendations |
| Free mini-check replaces paid demand | Validation results become misleading | Limit scope, quantity, and availability |
| Paid ads produce cheap but weak leads | CPL hides poor unit economics | Optimize for qualified locations and paid conversion |
| Report production is too slow | Service cannot scale profitably | Standardize templates, sources, and research workflow |
| Partner incentives create bias | Buyer-side trust may weaken | Disclose relationships and avoid transaction-dependent incentives |

## Go/No-Go Logic

Rumper should increase acquisition investment only after it has:

- A segment that repeatedly submits real locations
- A message that converts that segment near the pre-DP moment
- A paid offer that reaches the target AOV
- At least one channel with a plausible CAC below the validation threshold
- A report process that meets production-time and satisfaction gates

Until then, the GTM team should optimize for learning quality and paid evidence, not follower count, reach, or lead volume alone.
