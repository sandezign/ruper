# Rumper 30-Day Pre-Launch Validation Plan

## Purpose

This plan runs before the formal paid launch. It turns Rumper's current product strategy into a focused concierge validation sprint.

The central question is:

> Will first-time homebuyers with a real shortlisted property submit its location and pay for an independent location-risk audit before booking fee or DP?

The sprint should produce paid evidence, customer language, a usable report format, and an operational baseline. It should not be used to justify building the future self-serve platform before the validation gates are met.

## Strategic Boundary

### Included

- First-time homebuyers in Jabodetabek with a specific shortlisted property
- Buyers planning to decide within three months
- Mini-checks, paid beta audits, and two-location comparison tests
- Manual or semi-manual research and PDF delivery
- Interviews, usability observation, willingness-to-pay tests, and production-time measurement

### Excluded

- Interactive maps or dashboards
- Customer accounts and order tracking portals
- Automated scoring or report generation
- Property-price forecasts, investment returns, legal checks, developer reviews, or KPR simulations
- Broad awareness campaigns without a measurable path to qualified locations

## 30-Day Outcomes

### Early Validation Targets

These targets determine whether Rumper has enough signal to enter the formal launch period.

| Metric | Target |
|---|---:|
| Target-customer conversations | 10-15 |
| Qualified leads | At least 10 |
| Real property locations submitted | At least 5 |
| Mini-checks or beta reports delivered | 5-10 |
| Paid beta customers | 2-3 minimum; 3-5 preferred |
| Strong testimonials or case-study permissions | 2-3 |
| Customers rating the output above 8/10 | At least 80% |
| Average Comprehensive report production time | Below 2.5 hours |

The lower beta price is an early willingness-to-pay test. It does not replace the formal validation gate of at least Rp300,000 AOV. The launch phase must test improved packaging, especially the Comprehensive and Dual-Location offers, to reach that gate.

### North Star and OMTM

- **North Star Metric:** number of home-purchase decisions meaningfully supported by Rumper
- **30-day OMTM:** number of paid beta audits purchased by qualified buyers

## Core Hypotheses

| Priority | Hypothesis | Test | Success signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Buyers near DP experience urgent location-risk anxiety | Interview qualified buyers | At least 60% describe a concrete unresolved risk |
| 2 | Buyers trust Rumper with a real shortlisted location | Offer a limited mini-check | At least 5 qualified real locations submitted |
| 3 | Rumper reveals useful decision evidence | Deliver and observe mini-check usage | At least 80% rate it above 8/10 |
| 4 | Buyers will pay for deeper analysis | Offer a paid audit immediately after a mini-check | At least 2-3 paid beta purchases |
| 5 | Comparison creates higher perceived value | Offer two-location comparison to relevant buyers | At least one serious purchase signal |
| 6 | Concierge delivery is operationally viable | Time every production stage | Average below 2.5 hours |

## Week 1: Prepare and Recruit

### Objective

Prepare only the assets required to recruit qualified users and deliver the first mini-checks.

### Actions

1. Finalize one primary offer and CTA: `Cek Risiko Lokasi Rumah Saya`.
2. Prepare a short intake form covering location, intended use, DP timeline, primary concern, and contact permission.
3. Create a 3-5 page mobile-readable sample report or mini-check template.
4. Prepare the qualification, interview, delivery, and paid-offer WhatsApp scripts.
5. Create a validation tracker covering source, segment, concern, funnel stage, price offered, rejection reason, satisfaction, and production time.
6. Recruit through direct outreach, property communities, and a small number of trusted partners.

### Week 1 Target

| Metric | Target |
|---|---:|
| Relevant outreach | 20-30 |
| Target-customer conversations | At least 5 |
| Qualified real locations | 3-5 |

## Week 2: Deliver and Test Decision Value

### Objective

Learn whether the report is understandable, useful, and meaningfully different from a buyer's own research.

### Actions

1. Select the first three qualified locations.
2. Produce and deliver three mini-checks.
3. Measure research, analysis, writing, quality-control, and delivery time separately.
4. Observe which section each user reads first and what they share with family.
5. Revise the report after each delivery rather than waiting until the end of the week.

### UX Validation Questions

Within two minutes of opening the report, the user should be able to answer:

1. What is the most important location risk?
2. How reliable is the supporting evidence?
3. What should be verified during the physical survey?
4. Should the buyer proceed, reconsider, or investigate further?
5. Can the buyer explain the recommendation to a spouse or family member?

### Report UX Requirements

- Put the main recommendation and top risks first.
- Use one clear primary recommendation.
- Pair every risk color with an icon and text label.
- Show confidence beside every major finding.
- Provide an explicit `what to do next` section.
- Keep the report readable on a phone opened through WhatsApp.
- Avoid absolute claims and clearly state evidence gaps.

### Week 2 Target

| Metric | Target |
|---|---:|
| Mini-checks delivered | 3 |
| Satisfaction above 8/10 | At least 80% |
| Users identifying a useful or unexpected finding | At least 2 |
| Production-time baseline | Recorded for every report |

## Week 3: Test Willingness to Pay

### Objective

Test paid behavior rather than hypothetical interest.

### Actions

1. Offer a paid beta audit immediately after discussing each mini-check.
2. Test a Single-Location beta offer and a higher-value Dual-Location comparison.
3. Ask for an actual purchase and record the offered price.
4. Record rejection reasons verbatim.
5. Deliver paid reports first and request a testimonial or case-study permission after delivery.

### Suggested Price Tests

| Offer | Purpose | Suggested test range |
|---|---|---:|
| Single-Location Beta Audit | Test basic willingness to pay | Rp99,000-Rp149,000 |
| Dual-Location Comparison | Test higher-value packaging and AOV | Rp299,000-Rp349,000 |

Price tests must not be interpreted in isolation. Record the buyer's urgency, trust level, property value, alternative considered, and reason for accepting or rejecting the offer.

### Week 3 Target

| Metric | Target |
|---|---:|
| Paid beta customers | 2-3 minimum |
| Paid or additional beta reports delivered | 3-5 |
| Rejection reasons captured | Every declined offer |
| Strong testimonials | At least 1 |

## Week 4: Consolidate Learning and Decide

### Objective

Determine whether Rumper should enter the formal launch, iterate on a weak constraint, or pause.

### Actions

1. Complete follow-up reports and feedback collection.
2. Analyze the full funnel by segment, pain, message, channel, and offer.
3. Identify the strongest entry pain: pre-DP anxiety, flood, commute, access, or family decision support.
4. Update the report template, research checklist, scoring guidance, and customer-service scripts.
5. Document the repeatable production workflow and its bottlenecks.
6. Select the primary launch segment, message, offer, and acquisition channel.

### Decision Rules

**Proceed to formal launch when:**

- At least 10 qualified buyers have been engaged.
- At least 5 real property locations have been submitted.
- At least 2-3 qualified buyers have paid.
- At least 80% of customers rate the output above 8/10.
- Average Comprehensive report production time is below 2.5 hours.
- At least one pain, message, and acquisition path produces credible paid intent.

**Iterate before launch when:**

- Buyers submit locations but do not pay.
- Users value the report but cannot distinguish it from free research.
- Production takes too long.
- Trust or evidence quality blocks conversion.
- Leads are interested but not close enough to a DP decision.

**Pause or reconsider the segment when:**

- Fewer than five qualified buyers submit a real location after meaningful outreach.
- No user reports that the output affected their decision or next action.
- No credible willingness-to-pay signal appears.
- The service cannot be delivered consistently within a viable scope.

## Validation Funnel

Track this funnel weekly:

| Stage | Required learning |
|---|---|
| Relevant reach or outreach | Can Rumper reach buyers near a real decision? |
| Conversation | Does the problem feel urgent in the buyer's own words? |
| Qualified lead | Does the buyer match the initial segment? |
| Real location submitted | Does the buyer trust Rumper enough to share a candidate? |
| Mini-check delivered | Does the output create useful decision evidence? |
| Paid audit purchased | Will the buyer pay for deeper analysis? |
| Second location or referral requested | Is the value repeatable and recommendable? |

## Weekly Operating Rhythm

Assuming approximately 10 hours per week:

| Activity | Hours per week |
|---|---:|
| Outreach and qualification | 2 |
| Interviews and feedback | 1 |
| Report production | 4 |
| Research and quality control | 1 |
| Content or community activity | 1 |
| Metrics review and process improvement | 1 |

Every week must produce at least one paid conversion, one delivered report, or one validated learning that changes the offer, segment, report, or funnel.

## Pre-Launch Handoff

At the end of Day 30, record:

- Validated priority segment and strongest pain
- Winning message and CTA
- Paid conversion and rejection evidence
- Recommended launch packages and prices
- Best-performing acquisition channel
- Report satisfaction and usability findings
- Average production time and main bottleneck
- Updated SOP, report template, and scoring guidance
- Explicit `launch`, `iterate`, or `pause` decision

The formal launch begins only after this handoff is reviewed. The next validation target remains the existing launch gate: at least 10 paid reports, CAC below Rp150,000, AOV of at least Rp300,000, production time below 2.5 hours, and at least 80% satisfaction above 8/10.
