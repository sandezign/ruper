# Rumper Guerrilla Market Validation Plan

## Purpose

This plan converts low-cost guerrilla marketing tactics into measurable market-validation experiments for Rumper.

It complements the [30-Day Pre-Launch Validation Plan](./30-day-pre-launch-validation-plan.md). The existing plan remains the source of truth for the overall validation funnel, report delivery, and launch decision. This document focuses on finding the fastest path from a guerrilla tactic to a qualified buyer, a real submitted location, and a paid audit.

The primary validation question is:

> Will first-time homebuyers in Jabodetabek who have a shortlisted property and expect to decide within three months submit its real location and pay for an independent location-risk audit before booking fee or DP?

## Validation Principles

1. Measure behavior, not attention or stated interest.
2. Count a lead as qualified only when they have a real property candidate and a near-term decision.
3. Use free checks only as a limited trust-building step toward a paid offer.
4. Ask for payment or another meaningful commitment early.
5. Run no more than two active experiments at once.
6. Change one major variable at a time: audience, message, channel, or offer.
7. Preserve Rumper's buyer-side independence in every partnership and public case study.

## Core XYZ Hypotheses

| ID | Hypothesis | Passing signal |
|---|---|---|
| H1: Problem and timing | At least 30% of qualified buyers reached directly will submit a real shortlisted location for review. | At least 6 real locations from 20 qualified conversations |
| H2: Willingness to pay | At least 25% of qualified buyers who receive a mini-check will purchase a paid audit. | At least 2 paid purchases from 8 mini-check recipients |
| H3: Message | At least 10% of relevant people who respond to a pre-DP risk message will submit a real location. | At least 3 location submissions from 30 relevant responders |
| H4: Referral channel | At least 20% of qualified partner referrals will purchase a paid audit. | At least 2 paid purchases from 10 qualified referrals |
| H5: Value and advocacy | At least 50% of paid customers will take a concrete decision action or introduce one relevant buyer. | Decision action or qualified referral from at least half of paid customers |

These thresholds are initial decision rules, not universal market benchmarks. Revise them only after a complete experiment cycle, not during a weak run.

## Review of Proposed Tactics

| # | Tactic | Validation value | Main risk | Recommendation |
|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Free 5-slot weekly check | High: can generate real locations and paid follow-on offers | Attracts freebie seekers and consumes production time | **Run first**, but limit to two mini-checks per week and qualify applicants |
| 2 | Valuable comments in property groups | Medium: tests language and creates warm conversations | Easy to confuse engagement with demand | Use comments to start tracked DMs, not as a standalone success metric |
| 3 | Before-DP mini checklist | Medium: tests whether the pre-DP framing creates action | Downloads are weak evidence | Require a real location or WhatsApp conversation as the next step |
| 4 | Anonymous case study | High: demonstrates product value and evidence quality | Can feel vague or raise privacy concerns | **Run early** with explicit permission and a clear CTA |
| 5 | Location red-flag poll | Low: useful for message research | Opinion data does not prove urgency or payment | Use only to choose content angles; do not count as validation |
| 6 | Personal DMs to network | Very high: fastest route to qualified conversations | Friends may be polite rather than genuinely interested | **Run first**; ask for introductions to active buyers, not feedback |
| 7 | Public listing audit | High: makes the value proposition concrete | Defamation, privacy, and unsupported-claim risk | Run with anonymized details, evidence labels, and neutral language |
| 8 | LinkedIn UX thread | Low to medium: supports founder credibility | Audience may include peers rather than buyers | Defer unless it produces introductions to active buyers or partners |
| 9 | Small KPR advisor partnership | Very high: reaches buyers near a decision | Referral incentives can weaken perceived independence | **Test early** with transparent, non-exclusive referral terms |
| 10 | Simple landing waitlist | Medium as infrastructure; low as a standalone test | Email sign-ups overstate demand | Replace generic waitlist CTA with `Submit lokasi` and a paid-beta CTA |
| 11 | Offer for young couples | High: tests the Almost-DP Couple segment and family-decision job | Segment may be too broad without decision timing | **Test as a message variant** among qualified near-DP buyers |
| 12 | Area-specific content | Medium to high: captures specific search and concern intent | Can attract general residents and researchers | Test one area at a time with a location-submission CTA |
| 13 | Public survey | Low for market validation; useful for language discovery | Stated fears do not prove buying behavior | Use only if responses feed outreach or message design |
| 14 | 30-minute office hour | High: tests urgency, trust, and consultation demand | Can become free general education | **Test early**; require a real address and decision timeline to join |

## Prioritization

### Tier 1: Run During the First 30 Days

These tactics have the shortest path to qualified locations and paid behavior:

1. Personal DMs requesting introductions to active buyers
2. Limited free mini-check with immediate paid follow-on offer
3. Anonymous case study with `Cek lokasi saya` CTA
4. Qualified office hour requiring a real property candidate
5. Small KPR advisor or financial-planner referral test
6. Young-couple message variant for Almost-DP buyers

### Tier 2: Use as Supporting Acquisition

Use these only when every response can be attributed and moved into the validation funnel:

- Valuable group comments
- Before-DP mini checklist
- Public listing audit
- Area-specific content
- Landing page with location-submission and paid-beta CTAs

### Tier 3: Defer or Use Only for Message Research

- Red-flag polls
- Broad public survey
- General LinkedIn UX thread

These can generate useful language, but they should not consume time needed for qualified conversations, report delivery, and paid asks.

## Four-Week Experiment Plan

### Week 1: Test Direct Access and Qualification

**Active experiments**

| Experiment | Method | Primary metric | Pass threshold |
|---|---|---|---:|
| Personal introduction sprint | Send 30 personal messages asking for an introduction to someone with a shortlisted home and a decision within three months | Qualified conversations | At least 6 |
| Two-slot mini-check | Offer two qualified applicants a narrow free check, then immediately offer the paid audit | Real locations submitted | At least 2 |

**Required learning**

- Which words active buyers use for their strongest fear
- Whether `sebelum DP`, `cek risiko lokasi`, or `second opinion` creates the strongest response
- Why qualified buyers accept or decline the paid next step

### Week 2: Test Proof and Segment Message

**Active experiments**

| Experiment | Method | Primary metric | Pass threshold |
|---|---|---|---:|
| Anonymous case study | Publish one concise case showing a surprising risk, evidence, and next action; use a tracked `Cek lokasi saya` CTA | Qualified location submissions | At least 3 |
| Young-couple message test | Send two message variants to comparable qualified prospects: general pre-DP risk versus family-decision support | Qualified reply rate | Winning variant is at least 1.5x the other |

**Required learning**

- Whether proof increases trust enough for people to share a real location
- Whether decision support for couples or family is a stronger entry pain than general risk reduction

### Week 3: Test Borrowed Trust and Paid Intent

**Active experiments**

| Experiment | Method | Primary metric | Pass threshold |
|---|---|---|---:|
| Partner referral test | Recruit two small independent KPR advisors or financial planners; give each a clear qualification script and tracked referral path | Qualified referrals | At least 5 total |
| Qualified office hour | Hold one 30-minute session for up to three buyers; require a real location and near-term decision; offer paid audits before closing | Paid conversion | At least 1 paid audit |

**Required learning**

- Whether partner trust improves qualified conversion
- Which objections remain after a live explanation
- Whether consultation should be included, optional, or separately priced

### Week 4: Repeat Winner and Decide

Repeat the highest-performing channel and message from Weeks 1-3. Do not introduce a new tactic.

| Decision metric | Target |
|---|---:|
| Qualified conversations | At least 10 total |
| Real locations submitted | At least 5 total |
| Mini-checks or beta reports delivered | At least 5 total |
| Paid beta customers | At least 2-3 |
| Paid conversion from mini-check recipients | At least 25% |
| Concrete decision actions or qualified referrals | At least 50% of paid customers |
| Winning acquisition path | At least one channel produces two qualified locations or one paid customer repeatedly |

## Experiment Operating Cards

### Card A: Personal Introduction Sprint

- **Hypothesis:** Warm introductions can reach active buyers faster than broad public content.
- **Audience:** Personal and professional network members who may know a first-time homebuyer.
- **Ask:** Introduce Rumper to one person who has shortlisted a house and plans to decide within three months.
- **Do not ask:** “What do you think of this idea?”
- **Success:** Six qualified conversations from 30 messages.
- **Stop or revise:** Fewer than three qualified conversations after 30 messages.

### Card B: Limited Mini-Check to Paid Audit

- **Hypothesis:** A narrow, useful preview creates enough trust for a qualified buyer to purchase a deeper audit.
- **Method:** Deliver one or two findings and a survey question; offer the paid audit in the same delivery conversation.
- **Success:** At least two purchases from eight qualified mini-check recipients.
- **Guardrail:** Do not deliver a report comprehensive enough to replace the paid offer.
- **Stop or revise:** Locations are submitted but no one pays; investigate differentiation, trust, scope, and price.

### Card C: Anonymous Case Study

- **Hypothesis:** Specific evidence and a surprising finding make the service understandable and credible.
- **Structure:** Initial assumption, evidence checked, unexpected red flag, recommended next action, CTA.
- **Success:** Three qualified location submissions from the first two case studies.
- **Guardrail:** Remove identifying details, disclose uncertainty, and avoid claims unsupported by evidence.

### Card D: Partner Referral Test

- **Hypothesis:** Trusted advisors can reach qualified buyers close to booking fee or DP.
- **Method:** Give two partners a one-paragraph explanation, qualification checklist, sample report, and tracked referral link or code.
- **Success:** Five qualified referrals and at least one paid customer.
- **Guardrail:** Disclose referral arrangements and ensure partners cannot influence findings or recommendations.

### Card E: Qualified Office Hour

- **Hypothesis:** Buyers with a real candidate will invest time and pay after seeing Rumper's evaluation framework live.
- **Entry requirement:** Real location, primary concern, and decision timeline.
- **Success:** At least two attendees show up and one purchases a paid audit.
- **Guardrail:** Discuss the validation approach and one example risk; do not provide a full free audit.

## Funnel and Tracking

Track every tactic using the same funnel:

`Relevant reach → response → qualified conversation → real location submitted → mini-check delivered → paid audit → concrete decision action or referral`

Minimum fields:

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Lead ID and date | Preserve the evidence chain |
| Source tactic and specific variant | Attribute outcomes correctly |
| Qualification status and reason | Separate active buyers from general interest |
| Property location submitted | Measure trust and real demand |
| Decision timeline and primary concern | Identify the strongest trigger |
| Offer and price shown | Interpret paid conversion |
| Funnel stage and next action | Prevent leads from stalling |
| Rejection reason, verbatim | Diagnose the actual constraint |
| Production time | Validate operational viability |
| Payment amount | Measure willingness to pay |
| Decision impact or referral | Measure real value |

## Weekly Review Rules

Every week, answer:

1. Which tactic produced the most qualified conversations?
2. Which tactic produced real locations, not just engagement?
3. Which message produced the highest qualified reply rate?
4. At which funnel stage did most qualified leads stop?
5. What were the top three rejection reasons in the buyers' own words?
6. Which one experiment should be repeated, revised, or stopped next week?

### Continue

Continue a tactic when it produces at least two qualified locations or one paid customer and the result appears repeatable.

### Revise

Revise a tactic when it produces qualified conversations but fails at one clear funnel step, such as location submission or payment.

### Stop

Stop a tactic when it generates only reach, likes, poll responses, or unqualified conversations after a complete test cycle.

## Recommended Initial Scripts

### Personal Introduction Ask

> Aku sedang riset layanan independen untuk membantu pembeli rumah pertama mengecek risiko lokasi sebelum booking fee atau DP. Kamu kenal satu orang yang sudah punya rumah incaran dan berencana memutuskan dalam tiga bulan ke depan? Aku ingin menawarkan cek awal terbatas dan belajar dari kasus nyata mereka.

### Mini-Check Offer

> Minggu ini Rumper membuka dua slot cek awal lokasi untuk pembeli rumah pertama yang sudah punya rumah incaran dan sedang mendekati keputusan. Cek awal membahas satu risiko utama dan hal yang perlu diverifikasi saat survei. Jika ingin analisis lengkap, tersedia audit berbayar setelah cek awal.

### Case-Study CTA

> Sudah punya rumah incaran? Kirim lokasi dan risiko yang paling membuat ragu. Rumper akan memberi tahu apakah kasusnya cocok untuk cek awal atau audit lokasi lengkap sebelum DP.

### Partner Referral Ask

> Rumper membantu pembeli rumah pertama mengecek banjir, commute, akses, dan red flag lokasi sebelum booking fee atau DP. Kami sedang mencari dua advisor independen untuk menguji referral terbatas kepada klien yang sudah punya rumah incaran. Semua temuan tetap independen dan tidak dapat dipengaruhi pihak referral.

## Final Recommendation

Do not run all 14 tactics at once. Start with direct introductions and a tightly scoped mini-check because they provide the fastest evidence of trust, urgency, and payment. Use case studies and qualified office hours to strengthen proof, then test one small partner channel. Treat polls, surveys, and broad content as message research until they produce real locations or paid customers.
