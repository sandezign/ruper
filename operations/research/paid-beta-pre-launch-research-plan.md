# Rumper Paid-Beta Pre-Launch UX Research Plan

## Document Control

| Item | Definition |
|---|---|
| Research stage | Pre-launch, after early validation and before formal launch |
| Recommended duration | 14 active days; extend to a maximum of 21 days only to close a defined evidence gap |
| Product under test | Concierge or semi-manual Rumper Location Risk Audit |
| Primary audience | First-time homebuyers in Jabodetabek with a specific shortlisted home |
| Decision moment | Before booking fee or down payment |
| Primary research decision | `Go`, `iterate`, or `no-go` for formal launch |
| Handoff owner | Research lead |
| Decision owners | Product, business, and operations leads |

## Purpose

This sprint determines whether Rumper has enough paid-customer, user-experience, business, and operational evidence to be handed off for formal launch.

The central question is:

> Will first-time homebuyers who are close to paying a booking fee or down payment pay at least Rp300,000 for an independent location-risk report, understand and trust the report, and use it to improve a real property decision?

This is a launch-readiness evidence checkpoint. It does not replace Rumper's earlier problem, segment, or concept validation.

## Research Boundary

### Included

- Paid Single-Location and Dual-Location report orders
- Qualified buyers with a real property candidate
- Intake, qualification, purchase, report delivery, feedback, and decision follow-up
- Report comprehension, perceived trust, decision usefulness, and shareability
- Actual acquisition cost, amount paid, and production time
- Concierge or semi-manual report operations

### Excluded

- Free participants counted as willingness-to-pay evidence
- Hypothetical purchase-intent questions counted as payment evidence
- Buyers without a specific property candidate
- Investors purchasing primarily for speculation
- Locations outside the initial coverage area
- Validation of a future self-serve platform, dashboard, or automated report generator

## Research Objectives

1. Determine whether qualified customers understand Rumper as an independent second opinion on location risk before DP.
2. Validate actual willingness to pay at an average order value of at least Rp300,000.
3. Determine whether the report is understandable, credible, actionable, and useful in a real decision.
4. Verify that report production can consistently remain below 2.5 hours without unacceptable quality loss.
5. Produce an auditable evidence packet for the formal-launch handoff decision.

## Core Hypotheses

| ID | Hypothesis | Required evidence | Passing signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| H1 | Buyers near DP recognize an urgent need for independent location-risk evidence | Qualification notes from paid customers | Customers describe a specific unresolved risk and a near-term decision |
| H2 | Qualified buyers will pay for the service | Payment record linked to a qualified customer and report | At least 10 paid reports and actual AOV of at least Rp300,000 |
| H3 | Customers can understand the report without researcher explanation | Usability review after independent reading | At least 80% can identify the top risk, evidence confidence, recommendation, and next action |
| H4 | The report changes or strengthens a real decision process | Post-decision follow-up | At least 5 paid customers report a concrete decision or verification action |
| H5 | Customers perceive strong value | Rating and qualitative feedback | At least 80% give a rating above 8/10, meaning 9 or 10 |
| H6 | Concierge delivery is operationally viable | Complete production logs | Average below 2.5 hours, with no recurring critical QA failure |

## Research Questions

### Experience and Comprehension

- Can customers explain the report's purpose after one independent read?
- Can they identify the most important risk, confidence level, recommendation, and next action?
- Which sections are most useful, confusing, too technical, or least trusted?
- Can customers use the report in a discussion with a spouse or family member?
- Does the recommendation feel clear without sounding like a guarantee?

### Willingness to Pay

- Will qualified customers complete a real purchase at or above the target price?
- Does the customer perceive the price as reasonable relative to the risk of a poor home-buying decision?
- Which objections prevent purchase: price, trust, urgency, unclear differentiation, or ability to research independently?
- Is there credible repeat-purchase or referral intent?

### Delivery Feasibility

- Which production stage consumes the most time?
- Can the report be standardized while preserving decision value and evidence quality?
- Can the team consistently deliver within 2.5 hours?
- Which data gaps, subjective judgments, or QA issues threaten trust?

### Decision Impact

- Did the report cause the customer to proceed, pause, survey again, negotiate, compare another property, or stop?
- Which finding or recommendation produced that action?
- Did the report increase confidence or reveal an unexpected risk?

## Participant Criteria

### Required

- Approximately 25-40 years old
- First-time homebuyer seeking a home to occupy
- Searching in Jabodetabek
- Has at least one specific shortlisted property
- Plans to pay a booking fee or DP within one to three months
- Will share a location link, development name, or usable address
- Pays for the beta report
- Agrees to provide post-delivery feedback

### Highest Priority

- Is close to paying a booking fee or DP
- Is comparing one to three homes
- Has a concrete concern such as flood, commute, road access, or neighborhood risk
- Needs evidence for a decision with a spouse or family member

### Exclude From Primary Sample

- Has no specific shortlisted home
- Is only exploring the property market
- Is buying primarily for speculative investment
- Will not provide a usable location
- Only wants free information
- Is outside initial service coverage

## Sample and Recruitment

The formal-launch evidence sample is a minimum of **10 paid reports from qualified customers**. More than one report may come from the same customer, but customer-level metrics must not treat repeat orders as unique customers.

Recruit through direct outreach, referrals, property communities, and small controlled channel tests. Track every lead from source through payment and follow-up.

Use this funnel:

`Relevant reach → conversation → qualified lead → location submitted → paid → report sent → feedback completed → decision follow-up`

## Method

### 1. Intake Analysis

Collect the property location, decision timeline, purpose of purchase, primary concern, important daily destinations, transport preference, and report expectations.

### 2. Qualification Interview

Run a 10-15 minute WhatsApp chat or call before accepting the order.

Core prompts:

1. Is the property for your own residence or investment?
2. How far have you progressed in the purchase process?
3. When do you expect to pay the booking fee or DP?
4. Which location risk makes you most uncertain?
5. What have you already checked?
6. Why do you need a second opinion?

### 3. Paid Transaction Test

Offer real packages and request real payment.

| Package | Price test | Research purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| Single-Location Report | Rp300,000 | Test minimum target AOV |
| Compare Two Locations | Rp500,000-Rp600,000 | Test higher-value comparison demand |
| Consultation add-on | Rp100,000-Rp150,000 | Test demand for direct explanation |

Discounted or free reports may support learning, but they must be labeled and excluded from the Rp300,000 AOV and paid-report launch gates.

### 4. Report Usability Review

Ask the customer to read the report independently before a 15-30 minute review. Do not explain the report before testing comprehension.

Ask the customer to identify:

- The most important location risk
- How reliable the supporting evidence is
- The recommendation
- What must be verified during a physical survey
- What they would tell a spouse or family member

Then collect a rating, unclear sections, trust concerns, value perception, and improvement requests.

### 5. Production Feasibility Tracking

Record time separately for:

- Intake review
- Desk research
- Analysis and judgment
- Report assembly
- Quality assurance
- Customer delivery and revisions

Record both total elapsed time and hands-on production time. The launch gate uses hands-on production time.

### 6. Post-Decision Follow-Up

Follow up three to seven days after delivery to capture real actions:

- Proceeded
- Paused or delayed
- Conducted another physical survey
- Verified a finding with local sources
- Negotiated
- Compared another property
- Stopped the purchase

## Evidence Standard

Every paid-beta case must have a unique `Lead ID` and a complete evidence chain.

| Evidence area | Required artifact | Admissibility rule |
|---|---|---|
| Qualification | Intake and qualification notes | Confirms target segment, real property, and decision timeline |
| Payment | Transaction record, package, and amount paid | Must be a completed real payment; discounts are labeled |
| Acquisition | Source and attributable channel cost | CAC calculation method must be documented |
| Production | Stage-level time log and issues | Must include all production stages |
| Delivery | Report version and delivery date | Final report must be linked to the case |
| Experience | Rating and interview notes | Rating collected after independent report reading |
| Comprehension | Task answers from usability review | Researcher must record pass or fail for each comprehension task |
| Decision impact | Follow-up note describing action | Must state a concrete action, not only positive sentiment |
| Testimonial | Approved quote and consent status | Usage permission must be explicit |
| Objection | Verbatim reason for decline or concern | Categorize only after preserving the original wording |

The strongest evidence chain is:

> A qualified customer pays, reads the report independently, understands its recommendation, takes a concrete decision action, and describes the report's value in their own words.

## Metrics and Launch Gates

### Formal-Launch Handoff Gates

| Metric | Gate | Calculation |
|---|---:|---|
| Paid reports | At least 10 | Completed paid reports; free reports excluded |
| Actual AOV | At least Rp300,000 | Total recognized paid-report revenue divided by paid orders |
| Qualified CAC | Below Rp150,000 | Attributable acquisition spend divided by new qualified paid customers |
| Production time | Average below 2.5 hours | Average hands-on time across completed paid reports |
| Satisfaction | At least 80% above 8/10 | Percentage of paid customers rating 9 or 10 |
| Decision impact | At least 5 customers | Unique paid customers reporting a concrete decision or verification action |
| Strong testimonials | At least 3 | Specific value or outcome statements with usage permission |

All five primary gates, paid reports, AOV, qualified CAC, production time, and satisfaction, are mandatory for a `Go` decision. Decision-impact evidence, testimonials, a repeatable SOP, and a stable report template are mandatory handoff requirements.

### Supporting UX Signals

- At least 80% identify the top risk, confidence, recommendation, and next action without explanation.
- At least 20% express credible repeat or referral intent.
- No recurring critical misunderstanding suggests that Rumper guarantees safety or certainty.
- No recurring accessibility or mobile-readability issue blocks report use.

## Sprint Schedule

### Days 1-2: Prepare

- Finalize this plan, report template, intake, qualification script, tracker, payment flow, feedback form, and consent language.
- Define report versioning and the production-time logging method.

### Days 3-7: Recruit and Deliver Batch 1

- Generate 20-30 relevant prospects.
- Qualify leads and close the first paid customers.
- Deliver the first reports and run usability reviews.
- Revise only issues supported by evidence; preserve report version history.

### Days 8-13: Deliver Batch 2 and Close Evidence Gaps

- Continue recruitment and controlled channel tests.
- Deliver remaining reports.
- Conduct usability reviews and post-decision follow-ups.
- Standardize recurring production work and document bottlenecks.

### Day 14: Synthesize and Decide

- Freeze the evidence set.
- Calculate gates and analyze findings by segment, pain, package, and channel.
- Prepare the handoff packet and hold the launch-readiness review.

### Optional Days 15-21: Defined Gap Closure

Extend only when the review identifies one measurable, realistically closable gap, such as reaching 10 paid reports or reducing production time. Do not extend to avoid a `no-go` decision.

## Data Capture

### Lead and Evidence Tracker

Minimum fields:

`Lead ID`, source, customer profile, property area, DP timeline, main concern, funnel status, package, offered price, amount paid, discount, attributable acquisition cost, production time by stage, report version, rating, comprehension result, decision impact, testimonial consent, repeat intent, and objection.

### Research Note Template

For each customer, record:

1. Purchase context and decision timeline
2. Main unresolved risk
3. Reason for buying
4. Objections before payment
5. Most valuable report section
6. Confusing or low-trust section
7. Comprehension task results
8. Decision impact
9. Verbatim quote and consent
10. Recommended product or process improvement

## Analysis Plan

1. Calculate all gates using paid qualified cases only.
2. Segment results by urgency, primary pain, package, and acquisition channel.
3. Compare accepted and declined offers to identify trust, urgency, value, and price patterns.
4. Separate satisfaction from comprehension and decision impact.
5. Review production-time outliers and recurring QA issues.
6. Triangulate behavioral evidence, transaction evidence, interview evidence, and operational evidence.
7. Maintain a finding log with evidence strength: `strong`, `directional`, or `insufficient`.

## Decision Framework

| Evidence condition | Decision |
|---|---|
| All primary gates and handoff requirements pass | `Go`: hand off to formal launch |
| Customers pay and value the report, but production exceeds 2.5 hours | `Iterate`: standardize scope and operations before launch |
| Satisfaction is high, but AOV is below Rp300,000 | `Iterate`: revise packaging, differentiation, and value framing |
| Qualified CAC is too high | `Iterate`: do not scale paid acquisition; revise channel and messaging |
| Many qualified leads submit locations but few pay | `Iterate`: investigate offer, trust, urgency, and price |
| Customers pay but comprehension or satisfaction is weak | `No-go` until report quality and expectations are corrected |
| No credible paid demand or decision impact emerges | `No-go`: reconsider segment, problem, or offer |

No single strong anecdote can override a failed primary gate. Any exception must be documented with its evidence, risk, owner, and deadline.

## Handoff Package

Formal launch may begin only after the decision owners review a complete handoff package containing:

- Gate scorecard with calculation notes
- De-identified case-level evidence tracker
- Paid conversion and rejection analysis
- Usability, comprehension, trust, and decision-impact findings
- Approved testimonials and consent status
- Recommended launch segment, message, packages, and prices
- Winning acquisition channel and qualified CAC calculation
- Stable report template and version history
- Report-production SOP and QA checklist
- Known evidence gaps, legal or trust risks, and mitigations
- Prioritized improvements with owners
- Explicit signed decision: `Go`, `iterate`, or `no-go`

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Interest or praise is mistaken for demand | Require real payment and keep hypothetical intent separate |
| Discounted beta orders inflate willingness-to-pay evidence | Label discounts and exclude them from the target-price gate where appropriate |
| Customers interpret the report as a safety guarantee | Avoid absolute claims; show confidence labels, evidence gaps, and verification actions |
| Long or technical reports reduce comprehension | Put an executive summary, primary recommendation, top risks, and next steps first |
| Researcher explanation masks report usability problems | Require independent reading before the usability review |
| Incomplete data reduces trust | State source, recency, coverage, confidence, and limitations for major findings |
| Cheap leads make CAC appear healthy but do not convert | Use qualified paid-customer CAC, not cost per lead |
| Production time is understated | Log all stages and separate hands-on time from elapsed time |
| Sensitive customer and property data is mishandled | Limit access, de-identify synthesis, and obtain explicit testimonial consent |

## Research Ethics and Customer Protection

- Explain that Rumper provides decision support, not a guarantee or legal, structural, or financial assessment.
- Collect only data needed to deliver and evaluate the service.
- De-identify participant details in research synthesis.
- Obtain explicit permission before using quotes, reports, names, or property details externally.
- Allow customers to decline testimonial use without affecting service.
- Report evidence limitations and uncertainty honestly.
