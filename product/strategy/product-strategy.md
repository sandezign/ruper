# Rumper Product Strategy

**Status:** Active hypothesis
**Strategy horizon:** 12-18 months
**Review cadence:** Quarterly
**Current stage:** Pre-launch concierge validation

## Executive Summary

Rumper is a buyer-side residential location-risk intelligence service. It helps first-time homebuyers evaluate hidden location risks before paying a booking fee or down payment.

Rumper's initial strategy is to win the pre-DP decision moment for first-time homebuyer couples in Jabodetabek through trusted, personalized concierge audits. It will validate decision impact, willingness to pay, repeatable acquisition, and operational viability before automating delivery or expanding into a broader platform.

**Strategic positioning:**

> Rumper is the buyer's independent second opinion on location risk before booking fee or DP.

**Strategic focus:**

> Build trust first. Automate proven value later.

## Strategic Diagnosis

### The Problem

First-time homebuyers make high-value, difficult-to-reverse decisions using incomplete and fragmented location information. Developer and agent claims emphasize benefits, while important risks such as flooding, painful commutes, poor road access, and environmental disturbances can remain unclear until after purchase.

Buyers can research independently, but doing so requires time, knowledge, and a consistent evaluation method. Information from maps, news, social media, government data, community discussions, and field observations is difficult to combine and assess.

### The Opportunity

The period immediately before booking fee or down payment combines high decision urgency, fear of regret, a real shortlisted property that can be analyzed, and remaining freedom to investigate, compare, negotiate, delay, or walk away.

Rumper can own this decision moment by becoming the calm, independent, evidence-based buyer-side second opinion.

### Primary Alternatives

| Alternative | Strength | Weakness Rumper can address |
|---|---|---|
| Manual research using maps and internet sources | Free and accessible | Fragmented, time-consuming, and difficult to interpret |
| Developer or agent claims | Convenient and property-specific | Incentive is to close the transaction |
| Family and friend advice | Trusted and familiar | Inconsistent and not always evidence-based |
| Asking residents during a survey | Local and practical | Limited by timing, questions asked, and individual perspective |
| Generic GIS or property tools | Data-rich | Often technical and not designed for a buyer decision |

## Product Strategy Canvas

### 1. Vision Alignment

Rumper's vision is:

> Every homebuyer in Indonesia can make a confident property decision based on independent and understandable location-risk evidence.

The initial strategy advances this vision by focusing on the customer segment, geography, and decision moment where independent evidence is most urgent and testable.

### 2. Market Segments

Markets are defined by the buyer's problem and decision context, not demographics alone.

#### Beachhead Segment

First-time homebuyer couples in Jabodetabek who:

- Have one to three shortlisted homes.
- Expect to make a booking-fee or down-payment decision within approximately 30 days.
- Need evidence to evaluate risks and align with a spouse or family.
- Lack the time, confidence, or method to perform thorough location research.

**Core JTBD:**

> When I am close to committing to a shortlisted home, help me understand and validate its location risks so I can decide with my partner or family without avoidable regret.

**Why this segment first:**

- The decision is urgent and financially meaningful.
- A real location makes the value test concrete.
- Couples and families benefit from a neutral decision artifact.
- Buyers with multiple candidates create a natural comparison and repeat-purchase opportunity.

#### Adjacent Segment 1: Commuter First Buyers

Buyers considering more affordable homes in satellite cities who need to understand the true daily commute, first-mile and last-mile access, and transport trade-offs.

#### Adjacent Segment 2: Young Families

Buyers prioritizing stable daily access, flood resilience, schools, healthcare, and long-term livability.

Adjacent segments should not drive product expansion until the beachhead segment is validated.

### 3. Relative Cost Position

Rumper competes on unique decision value, not the lowest price.

The relevant comparison is not only the cost of free research. It is also the buyer's time, uncertainty, and potential cost of choosing an unsuitable location. Pricing should reflect the value of independent evidence and decision clarity while remaining accessible relative to a booking fee and total property commitment.

During validation, Rumper will test price and packaging rather than assume a final willingness-to-pay level. Contribution margin must improve before the service scales.

### 4. Value Proposition

**What before:**
The buyer is excited but uncertain about a shortlisted home. Relevant location information is fragmented, sales claims may be biased, and the couple or family lacks a shared evaluation method.

**How Rumper helps:**
Rumper converts available location evidence and the buyer's priorities into a personalized risk audit, confidence-labeled findings, practical recommendation, and field-survey checklist.

**What after:**
The buyer can proceed, investigate further, compare another property, negotiate, or walk away with greater confidence and a clearer rationale.

**Alternatives:**
Manual research, developer and agent claims, family advice, and informal field-survey conversations.

**Customer-facing promise:**

> Lebih yakin sebelum DP.

**Customer-facing explanation:**

> Rumper membantu mengecek risiko banjir, commute, akses jalan, dan red flag lokasi sebelum Anda membayar booking fee atau DP.

### 5. Strategic Trade-Offs

For the current strategy horizon, Rumper will not:

- Become a listing marketplace or property sales agent.
- Recommend properties based on commission.
- Serve developers as the primary customer.
- Guarantee that a location is safe or risk-free.
- Replace legal due diligence, building inspection, or physical surveys.
- Provide broad area recommendations before the buyer has a candidate.
- Cover all Indonesian cities before the Jabodetabek model is repeatable.
- Build a fully automated customer platform before demand and workflow are validated.
- Add property-price forecasts, investment returns, KPR simulation, or developer reviews to the core offer.

These choices preserve independence, trust, focus, and operational learning.

### 6. Key Metrics

#### North Star Metric

> Number of home-purchase decisions meaningfully supported by Rumper.

A decision is meaningfully supported when a qualified buyer confirms that Rumper caused or validated a relevant action, such as proceeding with greater confidence, investigating a risk, comparing another property, delaying commitment, negotiating, or walking away.

#### Current OMTM

> Number of paid beta audits purchased and used by qualified buyers before a real booking-fee or down-payment decision.

#### Supporting Metrics

| Customer value | Business health | Operational health |
|---|---|---|
| Decision-impact rate | Qualified lead-to-paid conversion | Production time by report stage |
| Satisfaction above 8/10 | Average order value | Quality-control failure rate |
| Useful or unexpected finding rate | Customer acquisition cost | Evidence reuse rate |
| Second-location or referral request | Contribution margin per report | On-time delivery rate |

### 7. Growth Strategy

Rumper begins as a founder-led concierge service. Growth should come from qualified conversations and trust-rich channels rather than broad traffic.

#### Initial Acquisition Channels

1. Direct outreach to buyers near a real decision.
2. Property and KPR communities.
3. Educational content about overlooked location risks.
4. Referrals from satisfied buyers.
5. Select buyer-aligned partners such as KPR advisors or independent property consultants.

#### Growth Sequence

```text
Founder-led validation
→ Repeatable trust and referral channels
→ Content and search acquisition
→ Self-service intake and assisted delivery
→ Selected partner distribution
```

Rumper should scale a channel only when it produces qualified locations, paid decisions, and acceptable acquisition economics.

### 8. Required Capabilities

#### Capabilities Rumper Must Build

- Buyer-centered location-risk research methodology.
- Personalized risk prioritization and scoring guidance.
- Evidence sourcing, confidence assessment, and traceability.
- Clear decision communication and mobile-readable reports.
- Consistent quality control and responsible recommendation language.
- Customer discovery, qualification, and decision-impact measurement.
- Reusable location evidence and structured internal data.
- Workflow automation based on proven operational bottlenecks.

#### Capabilities Rumper Can Partner For

- Specialized or licensed geospatial datasets.
- Payment processing and customer communications infrastructure.
- Legal, structural, or building inspection outside Rumper's scope.
- Trusted referral distribution where partner incentives align with buyers.

### 9. Can't/Won't and Defensibility

An individual report, checklist, or score can be copied. Rumper's defensibility must develop from a reinforcing system that is difficult or unattractive for alternatives to reproduce.

#### Emerging Defensibility

- A structured evidence library built from repeated location analysis.
- Buyer-priority and decision-impact data that improves personalization.
- Operational knowledge about which signals matter and how to validate them.
- Trust earned through buyer-side independence and transparent uncertainty.
- Reusable workflows that improve speed without reducing evidence quality.
- Referral relationships and brand ownership of the pre-DP decision moment.

#### Can't/Won't Advantage

Seller-aligned agents, developers, and marketplaces may be unwilling to emphasize negative location evidence because it can reduce transaction completion. Rumper's buyer-side independence allows it to surface risks and evidence gaps consistently.

This advantage only remains credible if Rumper protects its independence and avoids revenue models that reward property sales.

## Strategic Objectives

| Objective | Evidence of success |
|---|---|
| Prove buyers will pay for independent location-risk analysis | Paid purchases from qualified buyers near a real decision |
| Prove Rumper affects decisions, not only satisfaction | At least 60% of paid customers report a changed or validated next action |
| Establish repeatable, viable concierge delivery | Stable quality, improving production time, and positive contribution margin |
| Build reusable location-risk intelligence | Increasing evidence reuse without reducing relevance or confidence |

Targets and experiment details belong in the [validation plan](../../business/validation/30-day-pre-launch-validation-plan.md) and [product roadmap](../roadmap/README.md).

## Critical Hypotheses and Experiments

| Hypothesis | Low-effort experiment | Success signal |
|---|---|---|
| Buyers near DP experience urgent location-risk anxiety | Interview buyers with a real shortlisted property | At least 60% describe a concrete unresolved risk |
| Qualified buyers trust Rumper with a real location | Offer a limited mini-check | At least five qualified locations are submitted |
| Rumper provides value beyond manual research | Ask what findings were new and observe report usage | At least half identify meaningful new evidence or action |
| Buyers will pay for independent analysis | Ask for payment before producing the full audit | Paid beta purchases from qualified buyers |
| Reports influence real decisions | Follow up after the decision deadline | At least 60% report a changed or validated action |
| Comparison has stronger value | Offer single and dual-location packages | Credible paid demand for comparison |
| Delivery can become profitable | Track time and cost by production stage | Improving contribution margin without lower satisfaction |

## Strategy Coherence

The strategy reinforces itself:

1. Focusing on buyers near DP creates urgency and a measurable decision.
2. Concierge delivery enables direct learning and trust before automation.
3. Evidence-labeled reports differentiate Rumper from sales claims and generic tools.
4. Repeated reports create reusable evidence and operational knowledge.
5. Reuse and selective automation improve speed, margin, and customer experience.
6. Strong decision impact and independence build referrals and defensibility.

Automation, geographic expansion, and partner products are consequences of proven value. They are not substitutes for validation.

## Strategy Review Questions

Review quarterly:

1. Which customer segment, pain, and decision moment produce the strongest paid behavior?
2. Does Rumper meaningfully change or validate buyer actions?
3. Which report elements create value, and which add cost without impact?
4. Is buyer-side independence clear and credible?
5. Which acquisition channel reaches qualified buyers economically?
6. Which operational bottleneck is proven enough to automate?
7. Is the evidence library becoming more reusable and defensible?
8. Should Rumper launch, iterate, pause, or scale?
