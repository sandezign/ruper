# Phase 5 Plan: Scale and B2B Exploration

**Duration:** Minimum 20 weeks, then ongoing
**Capacity:** 200 gross hours per 20-week cycle; 160 planned hours; 40-hour buffer
**Primary owner:** Founder
**Phase goal:** Identify one scalable growth path without weakening buyer-side independence, product quality, or founder sustainability.

## Phase Constraints

- Test only one primary scale thesis per cycle.
- Maintain a maximum of 8 planned hours per week.
- Protect B2C delivery quality before partner experimentation.
- Accept only buyer-aligned partnerships.
- Do not expand geography until source and operational readiness are proven.

## Weekly Capacity Model

| Workstream | Hours/week |
|---|---:|
| B2C delivery, quality, and support | 2.5 |
| Primary growth-channel execution | 2 |
| Partner or intelligence experiment | 1.5 |
| Product and data improvement | 1 |
| Metrics and strategic review | 1 |
| Unplanned buffer | 2 |

## Month 1: Select One Scale Thesis

**Objective:** Choose the strongest growth path based on Phase 4 evidence.

### Candidate Theses

1. Scale direct B2C through content, search, referrals, or paid acquisition.
2. Scale buyer-aligned distribution through advisors or communities.
3. Create recurring value from area-level intelligence or structured evidence.

### Deliverables

- Channel and opportunity scorecard.
- Selected primary scale thesis.
- Partner independence criteria.
- Cycle metrics and stop conditions.

### Acceptance Criteria

- The thesis is supported by existing evidence.
- The experiment fits the weekly capacity ceiling.
- Stop conditions protect money, time, and independence.

## Month 2: Run the Primary Growth Experiment

**Objective:** Determine whether the selected path produces qualified, economically viable demand.

### Deliverables

- One focused acquisition or distribution experiment.
- Qualified-lead, paid-conversion, and cost data.
- Customer quality and decision-impact comparison.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Results are measured through paid decisions, not reach or impressions.
- Increased volume does not reduce report quality or delivery reliability.

## Month 3: Test One Partner or Intelligence Pilot

**Objective:** Test recurring value beyond direct one-off reports.

### Candidate Pilots

- Buyer-aligned KPR advisor referral workflow.
- Independent financial-planner or property-educator pilot.
- Area-risk insight brief.
- Structured evidence export for an approved partner.

### Deliverables

- Pilot agreement and independence guardrails.
- Pilot workflow or prototype.
- Usage, willingness-to-pay, and repeat-intent evidence.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Partner incentives align with the buyer.
- The pilot creates measurable recurring value.
- Partner work does not overload B2C delivery.

## Month 4: Improve the Winning Path

**Objective:** Strengthen the best-performing channel or pilot and reject weak paths.

### Priorities

1. Improve the strongest conversion or retention constraint.
2. Expand multi-location comparison only when demanded.
3. Improve reusable area evidence where it supports the winning path.
4. Reject experiments that lack paid or repeated behavior.

### Deliverables

- Improved winning workflow.
- Rejected-hypothesis record.
- Updated contribution-margin and capacity model.

## Month 5: Decide the Next Strategic Focus

**Objective:** Choose where Rumper should concentrate the next operating cycle.

### Decision Options

- Scale direct B2C.
- Develop buyer-aligned partner distribution.
- Develop recurring intelligence products.
- Maintain a profitable focused service.
- Pause expansion and improve the core.

### Deliverables

- Phase performance report.
- Next-cycle strategic focus.
- Updated roadmap and resource assumptions.
- Formal decision record.

## Critical Path

```text
Credible core product economics
→ One evidence-backed scale thesis
→ Focused growth experiment
→ One partner or intelligence pilot
→ Repeat and willingness-to-pay evidence
→ Strategic focus decision
```

## Exit Scorecard

| Evidence | Required threshold |
|---|---:|
| B2C volume | Sustained growth with viable economics |
| Primary acquisition channel | At least one scalable or sustainably focused channel |
| Satisfaction above 8/10 | At least 80% |
| Decision-impact rate | At least 60% |
| Contribution margin | Positive and stable |
| Buyer-aligned partner pilots | 1-3 focused pilots |
| Partner repeat use or willingness to pay | Demonstrated |
| Founder workload | Sustainable within capacity |

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Too many scale experiments dilute learning | Test one primary thesis per cycle |
| Partner incentives compromise independence | Apply written independence criteria before pilots |
| Increased volume exceeds founder capacity | Cap orders and improve price or workflow before growth |
| Geographic expansion reduces evidence quality | Require source and operational readiness checklist |
| B2B distraction weakens the consumer product | Limit partner work to 1.5 planned hours weekly |

## Phase Review Checklist

- [ ] One primary scale thesis was tested end to end.
- [ ] Paid or repeated behavior supports the winning path.
- [ ] Buyer-side independence remains credible.
- [ ] Customer quality and decision impact remain stable.
- [ ] Founder workload remains sustainable.
- [ ] Next strategic focus is recorded.
