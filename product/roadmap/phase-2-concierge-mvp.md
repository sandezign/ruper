# Phase 2 Plan: Concierge MVP

**Duration:** 12 weeks
**Capacity:** 120 gross hours; 96 planned hours; 24-hour buffer
**Primary owner:** Founder
**Phase goal:** Prove that Rumper can repeatedly acquire qualified buyers and deliver trusted paid audits with viable early economics.

## Phase Constraints

- Maximum planned work: 8 hours per week.
- Maximum active customer orders: 3.
- One primary acquisition channel test at a time.
- Delivery takes priority over content and new features.
- No customer-facing application development.

## Weekly Capacity Model

| Workstream | Hours/week |
|---|---:|
| Paid report production and quality control | 4 |
| Acquisition and sales | 1.5 |
| Customer follow-up and referrals | 1 |
| Operations and evidence-library improvement | 1 |
| Metrics and pricing review | 0.5 |
| Unplanned buffer | 2 |

## Month 1: Formalize the Service

**Objective:** Convert Phase 1 learning into a clear, sellable, repeatable offer.

### Priorities

1. Select one primary ICP, message, CTA, and offer.
2. Finalize Comprehensive and Dual-Location packages.
3. Standardize the order-to-follow-up workflow.
4. Publish one sample report and trust proof.

### Deliverables

- Formal package and pricing sheet.
- Final report and comparison templates.
- Approved order, research, scoring, quality-control, delivery, and follow-up SOPs.
- Public sample report.
- Basic funnel and unit-economics tracker.

### Acceptance Criteria

- A new qualified lead can understand the offer and next step without founder explanation.
- Every order follows the same status flow.
- Production time is recorded by stage.
- Package scope prevents uncontrolled custom work.

## Month 2: Prove Repeatable Delivery

**Objective:** Deliver paid reports consistently while maintaining decision value.

### Priorities

1. Complete paid Comprehensive and Dual-Location reports.
2. Enforce report scope and delivery SLA.
3. Build reusable evidence from repeated locations and risk categories.
4. Collect decision-impact evidence and referrals.

### Deliverables

- At least five additional paid reports.
- Reusable evidence-library structure.
- Weekly quality and bottleneck log.
- Testimonials and anonymized case studies.
- Referral request and second-location follow-up flow.

### Acceptance Criteria

- At least 90% of accepted orders meet the promised delivery date.
- Quality-control issues are recorded and declining.
- Customers can identify the key risk, confidence, and next action.

## Month 3: Prove Repeatable Acquisition and Economics

**Objective:** Identify one credible acquisition path and determine whether concierge delivery can become viable.

### Priorities

1. Concentrate on the best-performing acquisition channel.
2. Test improved packaging toward the target average order value.
3. Measure contribution margin and founder capacity.
4. Rank bottlenecks for possible Phase 3 automation.

### Deliverables

- Channel scorecard.
- Pricing and package decision.
- Contribution-margin baseline.
- Ranked automation opportunity list.
- Phase 2 decision record.

### Acceptance Criteria

- One channel repeatedly generates qualified real-location submissions.
- Customer acquisition cost and average order value are measurable.
- Repetitive bottlenecks are supported by time data, not intuition.

## Recurring Two-Week Operating Cycle

| Week | Focus |
|---|---|
| Week A | Acquire and qualify leads; deliver current orders; record time and quality |
| Week B | Deliver and follow up; improve one bottleneck; review channel and economics |

## Critical Path

```text
Clear offer
→ Qualified acquisition channel
→ Repeatable paid orders
→ Consistent delivery and quality
→ Decision impact and referrals
→ Viable economics
→ Ranked automation opportunities
```

## Exit Scorecard

| Evidence | Required threshold |
|---|---:|
| Paid Comprehensive or Dual-Location reports | At least 10 |
| Customer acquisition cost | Below Rp150,000 |
| Average order value | At least Rp300,000 |
| Satisfaction above 8/10 | At least 80% |
| Changed or validated customer action | At least 60% |
| Average Comprehensive production time | Below 2.5 hours |
| Repeat, second-location, or referral behavior | Demonstrated by multiple customers |

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Report demand exceeds weekly capacity | Cap active orders and publish realistic delivery times |
| Custom requests destroy repeatability | Enforce package scope and price exceptions explicitly |
| Content activity displaces customer delivery | Pause content when active orders reach the cap |
| One-off referrals look like a channel | Require repeated qualified leads before declaring channel fit |
| Low AOV makes concierge economics weak | Test comparison and consultation packaging |

## Phase Review Checklist

- [ ] At least one acquisition path has repeated paid evidence.
- [ ] Delivery SLA, quality, and decision impact meet thresholds.
- [ ] Unit economics and founder capacity are understood.
- [ ] Automation candidates are ranked by time saved and recurrence.
- [ ] Phase decision is recorded.
